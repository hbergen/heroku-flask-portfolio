About me

I'm a data scientist living in Seattle, Washington, and I currently work at Nordstrom, where I apply my skills in digital marketing.

  • 2002 to 2006: Undergraduate studies
    With minimal background in science or mathematics from high school, I decided that mathematics was a challenge I wanted to pursue in my undergraduate studies and switched my major from English to Mathematics. In 2006 I earned a B.S. in Mathematics from Columbia College, a small private teaching college in my hometown.
  • 2006-2009: Graduate studies
    In the Fall of 2006 I accepted a Teaching Assistantship with the University of Missouri and entered the M.S. program for Applied Mathematics. In 2009 I graduated from the program with a few hundred mathematical proofs, students, and beautifully typeset thesis pages behind me.
  • 2009 to 2015: Professional data diving
    After I completed my education, I spent the next few years gaining foundational experience working with data within a wide range of industries.

    I have applied my curiosity and attention to detail in industries ranging from real estate assessment, property insurance, biochemical defense, transportation safety research, and cancer research.
  • 2015 to now: Data science
    In mid-2015 I left the nonprofit research world and joined Nordstrom for the opportunity to increase my exposure to statistics applied in more of a business setting.

    In my current role as a Data Scientist at Nordstrom, I lead analyses from start to finish in support of marketing while leveraging new tools, refining documentation, and automating processes wherever possible.
